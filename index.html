What to try in Morocco:

In Morocco, you can try classic Arabic dishes - aromatic and spicy, incredibly delicious, always appetizing and very satisfying. However, the national cuisine of the country is in many ways unique. Let's tell you exactly what you need to try in Morocco in the first place. We will advise you what to buy at home to repeat these incredible dishes in your kitchen. Descriptions of Moroccan dishes with photos will help you not to get lost in this realm of flavors. You can get acquainted with the national cuisine in Morocco on your own or as part of gastronomic tours. In the first case, you can go to a restaurant of national cuisine or find something in the hotel. In the second case, everything is clear – the guide will help you find the best. We have compiled a list of the most interesting excursions in Russian in different cities of Morocco.

Couscous

In every Arab country, couscous is prepared in its own way. Even within Morocco, you can find various variations of the dish that are definitely worth trying. They are always based on the same cereal, vegetables, meat (lamb, chicken, sometimes beef, and sometimes all at once). Vegetables are cooked in broth with a stone, cut large so that they do not lose their shape. Cereals are traditionally steamed in special saucepans (couscous) – hence this unique taste. Vegetarians can try a vegetable version of the dish in Morocco. The most popular type of Moroccan couscous is couscous au sept légumes (with seven vegetables). Bring home spices (traders in the bazaars will tell you exactly what to take for a good couscous), some "right" cereals, and everything else you can easily find in Russia. If you really liked the dish, buy yourself a couscous dish to cook real couscous in Moroccan.

Bissara

Externally, Moroccan bean sauce (or mashed soup) beads does not look too attractive, but it's still worth trying. It is traditionally prepared in the cold season, but in restaurants of national cuisine and even in resort hotels in Morocco it can be found in any season. In fact, it is a paste of broad beans with lemon juice, hot pepper and cumin, generously poured with olive oil. If the dish is thick, it can be spread on bread. And if the mass is more like soup, then the bissara is still not eaten with a spoon – they just dip buns into it. It's really delicious! Bring concentrated lemon juice, a bottle of good olive oil, dried flat beans, cumin, red pepper from Morocco – you can easily cook bissara at home.
